To convert any color to a grayscale representation  first obtain the values of its red, green, and blue (RGB) primaries in linear intensity encoding . Then, add together 30% of the red value, 59% of the green value, and 11% of the blue value (these weights depend on the exact choice of the RGB primaries, but are typical).

So, for a pixel with RGB value (x,y,z) multipy x*30%, y*59% and z*11%. Add the new values for x, y and z and divide by 3, and replace the resultant value for all three components of the pixels RGB value, i.e. RGB(x,y,z) -->RGB(X,Y,Z)   where X=Y=Z=x*30% + y*59% + z*11%
